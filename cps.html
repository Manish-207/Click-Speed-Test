What is CPS Test?

The CPS Test (Clicks Per Second Test) is a simple way to check how fast you can click your mouse in a given time. It’s more of a fun challenge than anything serious, but it’s also helpful if you want to improve your speed and control—especially for gaming or tasks that need quick clicks.

This test works by counting your total clicks and dividing them by the time limit you choose. It gives you a clear score so you can track how you're doing and try to beat your best. Whether you're just curious or aiming to get faster, it's a great little tool to have fun with.

How to Measure Mouse Clicking Speed?

  1. Open Website: Go to CPSTest.io using your preferred web browser.

  2. Select Duration: Choose your test duration from the right side menu, default is 5 seconds.

  3. Start Test: Click the big button labeled "Click to Start Test" at the center.

  4. Click Rapidly: Begin clicking as fast as you can until the timer runs out.

  5. View Results: Your CPS score will appear, showing your clicks per second rate.

You can retake the click test by pressing the "Try Again" button to improve your mouse clicking speed. Regular practice will help increase your CPS score.

How to Increase Clicks Per Second Rate?

Want to click faster? Whether you're into gaming or just want to boost your reaction time, increasing your CPS (Clicks Per Second) can help. Here’s how you can get better at it.

Practice Every Day

Just like any other skill, clicking speed improves with daily practice. Spend a few minutes daily on tools like CPSTest.io to track your progress. Over time, your fingers get quicker, your muscle memory improves, and your CPS score climbs naturally.

Use a Gaming Mouse

If you’re serious about clicking faster, consider switching to a gaming mouse. These mice are built for speed — they’re more responsive, fit better in hand, and often come with click-friendly features like lightweight buttons and higher DPI.

Try Advanced Clicking Styles

Want to push even further? Learn and practice advanced clicking techniques. Here are a few that can seriously boost your CPS rate:

Jitter Clicking

Jitter clicking uses quick, controlled arm vibrations to click rapidly. It’s tricky at first, but with practice, it can give a solid CPS boost. Keep your hand still and use your forearm to generate those micro-movements.

Butterfly Clicking

Butterfly clicking uses two fingers (usually index and middle) to alternate clicks. This method often doubles your click rate. It feels odd at first, but with rhythm and timing, it becomes a reliable technique.

Drag Clicking

Drag clicking involves sliding your finger over the mouse button to generate many clicks at once. It’s fast — sometimes extremely fast — but requires the right mouse and can wear it down quicker.

Keep practicing and combine methods that suit you best. Your CPS will naturally improve over time — and your gaming performance will too.
